Blue lines

Positive weights push the next neuron upward when the source neuron is active.

Orange lines

Negative weights suppress the next neuron and reduce that pathway's influence.

Visibility

Brighter, thicker lines mean stronger active influence. Faint or missing lines are weak or inactive.

Output layer

Digit probabilities

The bars mirror the final 10-neuron output layer and update after every stroke.

Feature maps

How the colors are defined

Each thumbnail is a weight map. Bright green in Layer 1 and deeper green in Layer 2 indicate strong positive weights. Orange indicates strong negative weights. Black regions are close to zero, meaning that pixel contributes only weakly to that learned feature.

Layer 1 positive weights use bright green.
Layer 2 positive weights use deeper green.
Negative weights are shown in orange.
Black areas are near zero and contribute little.
